<p>I like to take photos and I love my reflex. When I bought my new mobile phone and I choosed a Lumia 920, my idea wasn't to use it as replacement for my reflex. I hate to take picture and share them &quot;as is&quot;, or via &quot;Istagram like&quot; apps that make all pictures &quot;similarly cool&quot;, so for a while I used my Lumia like a smarthone that just doesn't take photos:-)</p>  <p>Many on the web said that the Lumia camera is very good (to be a mobile phone...), so I was looking for a way to use it, but in my own way.    <br /> So the idea has been to apply the same &quot;workflow&quot; I used with my &quot;reflex&quot;, to the pictures taken with the Lumia.</p>  <p>With Canon 7D, I use only RAW, and at the end of the day, I move all the pictures on my laptop to export jpg via Lightroom 4.</p>  <p>How to obtain the same result with the Lumia? Well, I enabled the auto upload to SkyDrive of the pictures, and I used SkyDrive desktop (<a href="http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/skydrive/download)">http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/skydrive/download)</a> to have a copy of these pictures on laptop.</p>  <p><a title="Lumia-settings by Nicola since 1972, on Flickr" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/15216811@N06/9177661790/"><img alt="Lumia-settings" src="http://farm4.staticflickr.com/3786/9177661790_7b8792103d.jpg" width="300" height="500" /></a></p>  <p>In Lightroom I added the folder to the list of &quot;Monitored Foldeds&quot; so, automagically, the pictures arrive in Lightroom:-)</p>  <p><a title="LightroomSettings by Nicola since 1972, on Flickr" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/15216811@N06/9177663840/"><img alt="LightroomSettings" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7368/9177663840_5a0f27676e.jpg" width="249" height="202" /></a></p>  <p><strong><font color="#ff0000">And now the comparision</font></strong>. I have taken the same picture using:     <br /></p>  <ul>   <li>Lumia 920 ($499)</li>    <li>Canon 7D + Canon EF-S 10-22&#160;&#160; (&gt;$1600)</li> </ul>  <p>&#160;&#160; <br />The picture has been taken in Disneyland Paris few days ago. I have used almost the same post production on both picture: for Canon the source was a RAW, for Lumia a JPG.</p>  <p><strong>Canon 7D Version</strong></p>  <p><a title="Disneyland from my room - Canon 7D - ND0_4962 by Nicola since 1972, on Flickr" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/15216811@N06/9092444855/"><img alt="Disneyland from my room - Canon 7D - ND0_4962" src="http://farm3.staticflickr.com/2875/9092444855_1ab13de59f.jpg" width="500" height="281" /></a></p>  <p><strong>Lumia 920 Version</strong></p>  <p><a title="Disneyland from my room - Lumia 920 - WP_20130616_019 by Nicola since 1972, on Flickr" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/15216811@N06/9096007294/"><img alt="Disneyland from my room - Lumia 920 - WP_20130616_019" src="http://farm6.staticflickr.com/5335/9096007294_c15261d6a0.jpg" width="500" height="282" /></a></p>  <p>If you click on the images you can go to my Flickr photostream and see the picture at full resolution.    <br />In the following picture I show the 100% crop of a detail.</p>  <p><a title="100%-Crop by Nicola since 1972, on Flickr" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/15216811@N06/9175451413/"><img alt="100%-Crop" src="http://farm8.staticflickr.com/7303/9175451413_89c970f925.jpg" width="313" height="500" /></a></p>  <p><strong>My personal opinion</strong>: the Canon is better but the difference is not big: If you want to use the Lumia with a &quot;semi-professional&quot; workflow (i.e. using Lightroom), the result can be amazing.
