title: Microsoft Edge on mobile will now warn users of fake news

<img class="align-center" src="%20http://d2.alternativeto.net/dist/icons/newsguard_138664.png?width=36&amp;height=36&amp;mode=crop&amp;upscale=false">
<p><p>Microsoft is now labeling news websites that do not follow what it refers to as &quot;basic standards of accuracy and accountability&quot; within the Android and iOS versions of its flagship Edge browser.</p>
<p>Utilizing news authenticity rating service <a href='//alternativeto.net/software/newsguard/'><img alt='Small NewsGuard icon' class='mini-app-icon' src='//d2.alternativeto.net/dist/icons/newsguard_138664.png?width=36&height=36&mode=crop&upscale=false' />NewsGuard</a>, the mobile version of <a href='//alternativeto.net/software/microsoft-edge/'><img alt='Small Microsoft Edge icon' class='mini-app-icon' src='//d2.alternativeto.net/dist/icons/microsoft-edge_85645.png?width=36&height=36&mode=crop&upscale=false' />Microsoft Edge</a> is now automatically providing notifications about websites, such as <a href="https://www.dailymail.co.uk/" rel="nofollow">Daily Mail Online</a> and <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/" rel="nofollow">Breitbart</a>, that fail to meet certain quality standards. The notification reads:<br />
&quot;Proceed with caution: this website generally fails to maintain basic standards of accuracy and accountability.&quot;</p>
<p>In an interview with <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/media/2019/jan/23/dont-trust-daily-mail-website-microsoft-browser-warns-users" rel="nofollow">The Guardian</a>, NewsGuard co-founder Steve Brill stated that NewsGuard is happy to hold itself accountable to these ratings and that any complaints or disputes should go through them and not Microsoft. Brill stated “They can blame us. And we’re happy to be blamed. Unlike the platforms we’re happy to be accountable.&quot; NewsGuard differentiates itself by relying on journalists to review and give verdicts on sites, and not the usual algorithmically driven processes other information verification services may use.</p>
<p>NewsGuard hopes this rollout on Edge for <a href='//alternativeto.net/software/android/'><img alt='Small Android icon' class='mini-app-icon' src='//d2.alternativeto.net/dist/icons/android_84815.png?width=36&height=36&mode=crop&upscale=false' />Android</a> and <a href='//alternativeto.net/software/ios/'><img alt='Small iOS icon' class='mini-app-icon' src='//d2.alternativeto.net/dist/icons/ios_138506.png?width=36&height=36&mode=crop&upscale=false' />iOS</a> is the first of many on a variety of platforms that the service also supports, including <a href='//alternativeto.net/software/google-chrome/'><img alt='Small Google Chrome icon' class='mini-app-icon' src='//d2.alternativeto.net/dist/icons/google-chrome_96565.png?width=36&height=36&mode=crop&upscale=false' />Google Chrome</a> and <a href='//alternativeto.net/software/firefox/'><img alt='Small Mozilla Firefox icon' class='mini-app-icon' src='//d2.alternativeto.net/dist/icons/firefox_114558.png?width=36&height=36&mode=crop&upscale=false' />Mozilla Firefox</a>.</p>
