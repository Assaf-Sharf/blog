<img class="align-center" src="%20http://ifttt.com/images/no_image_card.png">
<p>A major update to the stocks messaging app for Android has been released, with Material Design-based revisions being implemented.</p><p>As [reported by Dami Lee at The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/2018/8/16/17698986/android-messages-dark-mode-google-material-design) and [Brittany Roston at SlashGear](https://www.slashgear.com/android-messages-gets-dark-mode-heres-how-to-enable-it-16541470/), the built-in messages app for the Android mobile operating system has received official support for a dark mode.</p><p>In addition to support for dark mode, other Material Design enhancements are also present, including new fonts for text, replacing the + icon with a "start chat" button when looking to message a new person, and defaulting the color for message bubbles to blue (while also removing the option to change the color of for message bubbles or contacts).</p><p>Version 3.5 of the https://alternativeto.net/software/messenger/ app is [available now](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.apps.messaging) on the https://alternativeto.net/software/android-market/.</p>
